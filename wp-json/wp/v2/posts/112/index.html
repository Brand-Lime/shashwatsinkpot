A creak masks the silence<\/p>\n\n\n\n

of solitude and head,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

When the poet comforts himself<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In an olden wooden bed.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Wordings laden ladder of<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Stupor which ends swirling,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

 He sinks down there,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Makes sentences of his feeling.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

With the brush of a quill<\/p>\n\n\n\n

He paints his thoughtful mind,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

With most colourful words<\/p>\n\n\n\n

On the canvas of parchment aligned.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

“Oh! Men and women,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Naive critics of the world,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Shall do praise wordless colours<\/p>\n\n\n\n

But not colourful words?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Like an alluringly painted canvas,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

 Are poetic words in a yellow hue,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Standing alone untouched Waiting for literates to walk through.”<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
