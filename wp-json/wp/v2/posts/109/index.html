Hail thou attire of monsoon,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Thy rhyme of restless rhapsodic tune,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Thy holy flair deluding Ahilya,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

By carol of the pearls hymning Indra.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

What sonneteer to pen down thee,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

What thy ode, what poesy?<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Here I sing thy unsung praise,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In ‘manly-tuned’ glorious ways.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Homecoming of thine incarnated overcast,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Crawling empyrean floor like a toddler surpassed,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Screening the age of hoary sky,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Like a young one gladdening grandpa by.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Then, in the sway of tyrannical wind,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Thou slant off the course vertically, grimmed,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Like a youth ‘enslaved’ by envious greed,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Sheerly  living as ‘master’ of breed.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

‘Tis not the end, not a frail thou art,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

As thunders & flashes happen to start,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

What were droplets, art now hail,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

No tyranny can hast them sail.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Thy eerie tempest o’er us, harass,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

And tame that wind as hours pass,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Likewise for a man, passes years,<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Truns he, bold & potent without fear.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Drizzle thou slowly farewell the skyway, Like a wise old man passes away.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
