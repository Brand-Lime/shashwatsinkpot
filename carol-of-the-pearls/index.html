Carol of the pearls

By
Shashwat Shekhar
-
October 7, 2020
36
4
WhatsApp
Facebook
Twitter
Print

    Hail thou attire of monsoon,

    Thy rhyme of restless rhapsodic tune,

    Thy holy flair deluding Ahilya,

    By carol of the pearls hymning Indra.

    What sonneteer to pen down thee,

    What thy ode, what poesy?

    Here I sing thy unsung praise,

    In ‘manly-tuned’ glorious ways.

    Homecoming of thine incarnated overcast,

    Crawling empyrean floor like a toddler surpassed,

    Screening the age of hoary sky,

    Like a young one gladdening grandpa by.

    Then, in the sway of tyrannical wind,

    Thou slant off the course vertically, grimmed,

    Like a youth ‘enslaved’ by envious greed,

    Sheerly  living as ‘master’ of breed.

    ‘Tis not the end, not a frail thou art,

    As thunders & flashes happen to start,

    What were droplets, art now hail,

    No tyranny can hast them sail.

    Thy eerie tempest o’er us, harass,

    And tame that wind as hours pass,

    Likewise for a man, passes years,

    Truns he, bold & potent without fear.

    Drizzle thou slowly farewell the skyway, Like a wise old man passes away.
